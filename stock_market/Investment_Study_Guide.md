## Chapter 1: Fundamentals of Investment

### 1.1 Personal Investment Planning

> [!question] Key Questions to Ask Before Investing
> - What is your personal investment goal?
> - How much do you already possess?
> - What industry and company combinations align with your needs?

#### Investment Goals Examples:
- **Retirement planning** - Long-term wealth accumulation
- **Down payment for house** - Medium-term savings goal
- **Education funding** - Targeted future expense
- **Emergency fund building** - Short-term liquidity needs
- **Wealth accumulation** - General investment growth

#### Current Financial Assessment:
- Current savings and assets
- Existing investments
- Net worth calculation
- Risk tolerance determination

### 1.2 Determining Risk Tolerance Level

> [!warning] Risk Tolerance Factors
> Your risk tolerance depends on multiple interconnected factors that should be carefully evaluated.

#### Personal Preference Factors:
- **Safety vs. Growth preference**
  - Conservative investors prefer capital preservation
  - Aggressive investors seek higher growth potential

#### Time Factors:
- **When you need your money**
  - Short-term needs require lower risk
  - Long-term goals allow for higher risk

#### Psychological Factors:
- **Market reaction tolerance**
  - How you handle market volatility
  - Emotional response to losses
  - Ability to stay invested during downturns

#### Financial Factors:
- **Existing debt obligations**
  - High debt reduces risk capacity
  - Debt-to-income ratio considerations
- **Alternative income sources**
  - Job security and stability
  - Multiple income streams
  - Emergency fund availability

> [!tip] Risk Tolerance Scale
> Rate your risk tolerance from 1-10 (10 being highest risk tolerance)

### 1.3 Investment Goal Setting (SMART Criteria)

Your investment goals should be **SMART**:

| Criteria | Description | Example |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| **S**pecific | Clearly defined objective | "Save for retirement" |
| **M**easurable | Quantifiable target amount | "$2 million" |
| **A**chievable | Realistic given circumstances | Based on income/timeline |
| **R**elevant | Aligned with life priorities | Matches personal values |
| **T**ime-Bound | Clear deadline or timeline | "By age 50" |

#### SMART Goal Examples:
- "Save $2 million for retirement by age 50" (25-year timeline)
- "Accumulate $100,000 for world tour in 5 years"
- "Build $200,000 for house down payment in 2 years"

### 1.4 Common Investing Products and Approaches

#### Mutual Funds
- **Pooled investment vehicles**
- Professional management
- Diversification benefits
- Higher fees compared to ETFs
- Active or passive management

#### Stocks
- **Individual company ownership**
- Direct equity investment
- Higher risk and potential return
- Requires more research and monitoring

#### Bonds
- **Fixed income securities**
- Government or corporate debt
- Lower risk, steady income
- Interest rate sensitivity

#### GICs (Guaranteed Investment Certificates)
- **Deposit with guaranteed return**
- Principal protection
- Similar to bank term deposits
- Longer maturity periods
- Safe investment option

#### ETFs (Exchange-Traded Funds)
> [!success] ETF Advantages
> ETFs combine the benefits of mutual funds with the flexibility of stock trading.

- **Pooled investments** like mutual funds
- Trade on stock exchanges
- Often mirror stock indices (**passive management**)
- **Lower fees** than mutual funds
- High liquidity
- Can be traded throughout trading day

### 1.5 Registered Plans in Canada

#### TFSA (Tax-Free Savings Account)
> [!money] Tax-Free Growth
> Earnings are completely tax-free, making this an excellent savings vehicle.

**Key Features:**
- Tax-free growth and withdrawals
- Individual contribution limits (cumulative)
- Flexible withdrawal without penalties
- Contribution room restored after withdrawal
- No age restrictions for withdrawals

#### RRSP (Registered Retirement Savings Plan)
> [!info] Tax Deferral Strategy
> RRSPs provide immediate tax relief but defer taxes until retirement when you may be in a lower tax bracket.

**Key Features:**
- Tax-deferred growth
- **Tax-deductible contributions**
- Designed for retirement savings
- **Contribution limit:** 18% of previous year's income (with caps)
- Tax paid upon withdrawal (typically in retirement)
- **Avoid lump-sum withdrawals** to prevent high tax brackets

**2024 Contribution Limit:** 18% of 2023 declared income

#### RESP (Registered Education Savings Plan)
> [!gift] Government Matching
> The government provides a 20% match on contributions, making this a valuable education savings tool.

**Key Features:**
- Education savings for children
- **20% government match** up to $500 annually
- **Lifetime government contribution:** $7,200
- **Lifetime contribution room:** $50,000
- Account active up to 31 years

#### FHSA (First Home Savings Account)
> [!home] Best of Both Worlds
> Combines the tax benefits of both RRSPs and TFSAs for first-time home buyers.

**Key Features:**
- Combines RRSP and TFSA benefits
- **Tax-deductible contributions**
- **Tax-free withdrawals** for first home
- Account active for **15 years** after opening
- **Annual limit:** $8,000
- **Lifetime limit:** $40,000
- Can be combined with partner's account

#### RRIF (Registered Retirement Income Fund)
**Key Features:**
- Converts from RRSP at retirement
- Flexible annual withdrawal amounts
- Mandatory minimum withdrawals
- Retirement income planning tool

### 1.6 Regulatory Bodies

#### Canadian Securities Administrator (CSA)
- Develops harmonized securities regulation
- National coordination of provincial regulations
- Investor protection focus

#### Alberta Securities Commission (ASC)
- Provincial regulatory agency
- Administers Alberta's securities laws
- Fosters fair and efficient capital markets
- Protects investors within Alberta

## Chapter 2: Technical Analysis

### 2.1 Understanding Market Trends

> [!chart] Trend Definition
> A trend is the general direction of market movement over time, characterized by successive peaks and troughs.

#### What is a Trend?
- Market movement direction over time
- **Not a straight line** - has ups and downs
- Identifies overall market direction

#### Uptrend Characteristics:
- Each successive peak **higher** than prior peak
- Each successive low **higher** than preceding low
- Indicates **bullish** market sentiment
- Suggests continued price appreciation

#### Trend Failure/Reversal:
- Failure to maintain higher peaks and lows
- Signals potential trend change
- May indicate market sentiment shift

#### Time Classifications:
| Trend Type | Duration | Use Case |
|------------|----------|----------|
| **Long-term** | 6 months to several years | Strategic planning |
| **Intermediate** | 1-6 months | Tactical adjustments |
| **Short-term** | Less than 1 month | Trading decisions |

> [!strategy] Strategic Implications
> - Identify trend type before making decisions
> - Major trends warrant different strategies than short-term movements
> - Trend reversal requires portfolio adjustment

### 2.2 Technical Analysis Tools

#### Trendlines
- **Lines drawn to connect price points**
- **Uptrend:** Line drawn below price action
- **Downtrend:** Line drawn above price action
- Shows support and resistance levels

#### Channel Lines
- **Two parallel lines** containing price movement
- Shows primary trend boundaries
- Indicates short-term fluctuations within trend

> [!note] Channel Width Implications
> - **Wide channels:** Weaker primary trend
> - **Narrow channels:** Sharp, strong trend
> - **Breakouts** from channels signal trend changes

### 2.3 Trading Rules and Strategies

#### Doubling and Halving Rule
> [!warning] Risk Management Strategy
> This rule helps overcome emotional biases and provides systematic position sizing.

**Rules:**
- **When stock doubles:** Sell at least partial position
- **When stock halves:** Consider buying more (after fundamental analysis)
- Helps overcome **endowment bias**
- Provides systematic approach to position sizing

**Important Notes:**
- Does NOT guarantee returns
- Should be combined with fundamental analysis
- Helps balance portfolio risk
- Prevents emotional decision-making

### 2.4 Stock Exchange Information

#### Key Information to Monitor:

| Metric | Description | Significance |
|--------|-------------|--------------|
| **Ticker Symbol** | Unique company identification | Trading and tracking |
| **Volume** | Number of shares traded | Higher volume = more activity |
| **Price Change** | Daily price fluctuation | Trend indication |
| **52-Week High/Low** | Annual price range | Volatility measurement |
| **Bid/Ask Spread** | Buyer vs. Seller prices | Liquidity indicator |

#### Liquidity Indicators:
- **Higher trading volume**
- **Lower bid/ask spreads**
- Easier to buy/sell positions

### 2.5 Chart Patterns

#### Support and Resistance
- **Support:** Price level where buying interest emerges
- **Resistance:** Price level where selling pressure appears
- Key levels for entry and exit decisions

#### Triangle Patterns

##### Symmetrical Triangle
- **Sideways price movement** narrowing over time
- Indicates **consolidation phase**
- **Trend continuation pattern**
- Breakout direction follows prior trend

##### Ascending Triangle
> [!bullish] Bullish Pattern
- **Flat upper resistance line**
- **Rising lower support line**
- Suggests **upward breakout** likely

##### Descending Triangle
> [!bearish] Bearish Pattern
- **Flat lower support line**
- **Falling upper resistance line**
- Suggests **downward breakout** likely

#### Other Important Patterns:
- Double/Triple tops and bottoms
- Head and shoulders patterns
- Flag and pennant formations

### 2.6 Technical Indicators

#### Moving Averages (Lagging Indicators)
- **Simple Moving Average (SMA)**
- **Weighted Moving Average (WMA)**
- **Exponential Moving Average (EMA)**
- Trend identification tools
- Smooth out price fluctuations

#### Envelopes and Bands
- Percentage envelopes
- **Bollinger Bands** (standard deviation based)
- Identify overbought/oversold conditions

#### Oscillators (Leading Indicators)
- **Relative Strength Index (RSI)**
- **Chande Momentum Oscillator**
- **Ultimate Oscillator** (weighted)
- Identify entry and exit points
- Show overbought/oversold conditions

#### MACD (Moving Average Convergence/Divergence)
> [!success] Best of Both Worlds
> MACD combines trend identification and oscillator functions in one indicator.

**MACD Components:**

1. **MACD Line (Blue):**
   - Formula: `Fast MA (12 days) - Slow MA (26 days)`
   - **Above zero:** Uptrend indication
   - **Below zero:** Downtrend indication

2. **Signal Line (Red):**
   - **9-day exponential moving average** of MACD
   - Provides trading signals through crossovers
   - **Bullish signal:** MACD crosses above signal line
   - **Bearish signal:** MACD crosses below signal line

3. **Histogram:**
   - Distance between MACD and signal line
   - Shows **trend strength**
   - Higher bars indicate stronger trends

> [!strategy] Popular MACD Strategy
> - **Buy:** MACD crosses above signal line below zero with high momentum
> - **Sell:** MACD crosses below signal line above zero

### 3.2 Asset Allocation Strategies

#### Risk-Based Allocation:
- **Conservative:** Higher bonds, lower stocks
- **Moderate:** Balanced stocks and bonds
- **Aggressive:** Higher stocks, lower bonds

#### Age-Based Rules:
- **Traditional:** Bond percentage = your age
- **Modern:** 100 minus age = stock percentage
- Adjust based on personal circumstances

#### Rebalancing:
- Regular portfolio review
- Maintain target allocations
- **Buy low, sell high** through rebalancing

### 3.3 Monthly Contribution Planning

#### Backward Calculation Method:
- Use compound interest calculators
- Determine required monthly contributions
- Assess goal achievability (**SMART criteria**)

#### Incremental Contribution Strategy:
> [!tip] Building Investment Habits
> Start small and increase over time to build sustainable investment habits.

- Start with manageable amount (**$200/month example**)
- **Increase contributions** when income rises
- Utilize bonuses and windfalls
- Build consistent investment habits

### 3.4 Passive vs. Active Investment Strategies

#### Passive Investment Strategy
> [!success] Passive Advantages
> **96.63%** of funds underperform the S&P/TSX Composite - only ~3% outperform the market.

**Characteristics:**
- Match market performance
- **Index ETF funds** focus
- Easy to manage
- Lower risk profile
- Consistent with market returns

**Reasons for Passive Approach:**
- Difficulty beating market consistently
- Lower fees and expenses
- Less time-intensive
- Proven long-term performance

#### Active Investment Strategy
**Characteristics:**
- Attempt to beat market performance
- Requires more research and monitoring
- Higher potential returns
- Higher risk profile
- More time and expertise required

### 3.6 Financial Priorities

> [!warning] Priority Order
> Follow this order to build a strong financial foundation before investing.

## Chapter 4: Corporate Actions and Stock Analysis

### 4.1 Dividend Policies

#### Cash Dividends
**Preferred by investors seeking:**
- Present consumption needs
- Regular income stream
- Immediate cash flow
- Lower volatility

**Characteristics:**
- Direct cash payment to shareholders
- Reduces company cash reserves
- Taxable income for investors
- Regular payment schedule

#### Stock Dividends
**Preferred by investors seeking:**
- Growth potential
- Immediate tax savings
- Capital gains over present consumption
- Long-term wealth building

**Market Effects:**
- Stock price dilution initially
- Potential demand increase
- Positive market forecast implications
- Enhanced liquidity

> [!warning] Cases Against Stock Dividends
> - Saturated business environments
> - Present consumption needs
> - Holding period restrictions
> - EPS dilution concerns

### 4.2 Stock Splits

> [!info] Stock Split Definition
> Division of existing shares into multiple new shares while maintaining total company value.

#### Purpose:
- Maintains total company value
- **Increases share affordability** and liquidity
- Attracts smaller retail investors
- Signals company confidence

#### Common Split Ratios:
- **2-for-1:** Each share becomes 2 shares
- **3-for-1:** Each share becomes 3 shares
- **4-for-1:** Each share becomes 4 shares

#### Stock Dividend vs. Stock Split Comparison:

| Aspect | Stock Dividend | Stock Split |
|--------|----------------|-------------|
| **Action** | Distribution of additional shares | Division of existing shares |
| **Ratio** | Typically 10% dividend | 2-for-1, 3-for-1 ratios |
| **Purpose** | Non-cash dividend, growth signal | Affordability/liquidity increase |
| **Regulation** | No regulatory interference | SEC regulation required |
| **Fractional Shares** | Can involve fractional shares | No fractional shares |
| **Market Signal** | Growth expectation | Higher demand expectation |

#### Historical Examples:
- **Amazon 1999:** 2-for-1 split
- **Google 2014:** 2-for-1 split
- **Apple 2014:** 7-for-1 split
- **Apple:** 5 total splits in history
- **NVIDIA:** 6 total splits (latest June 10, 2024)
- **Tesla 2020:** 5-for-1 split

#### Stock Split Impact Example:
> [!example] Split Calculation
> **Before Split:**
> - 1,000 shares at $10/share
> - Market cap: $10,000
> 
> **After 2-for-1 Split:**
> - 2,000 shares at $5/share
> - Same market cap: $10,000
> - More affordable price point
> - Increased demand potential
> - Price appreciation from $5 to $8
> - Better liquidity and trading volume

### 4.3 Reverse Stock Splits

> [!warning] Generally Negative Signal
> Reverse splits often indicate company distress and should be approached with caution.

#### Definition:
- **Reduction** in outstanding shares
- Examples: 1-for-2, 1-for-3 ratios
- Also called stock consolidation, merge, or rollback

#### Typical Reasons:
- Maintain stock exchange listing requirements
- Meet minimum price per share requirements
- Avoid penny stock classification
- Attract institutional investors

#### Market Perception:
- **Generally negative** market reaction
- Suggests company distress
- Artificial price inflation attempt
- No fundamental value creation

**Example:** Barnes & Noble Education: 1-for-100 reverse split in 2024

### 4.4 Investment Analysis Framework

#### Hyper-Growth Investment Strategy:
- Stock splits indicate strong growth potential
- Compound growth opportunities
- Higher risk, higher potential reward
- Requires careful company selection

> [!summary] Key Takeaways
> - **Cash dividends:** Present consumption preference
> - **Stock dividends:** Growth and wealth maximization focus
> - **Stock splits:** Exceptional growth potential in quality companies
> - **Reverse splits:** Generally avoid due to negative implications

## Chapter 5: Fundamental Analysis

### 5.1 Fundamental Analysis Structure

#### Quantitative Analysis:
- **Balance sheet** statement analysis
- **Cash flow** statement evaluation
- **Profit and loss** statement review
- Financial ratio analysis (**relative valuation**)
- **Intrinsic valuation** (DCF and CAPM models)

#### Qualitative Analysis:
- **Corporate governance** assessment
- **Competitive landscape** evaluation
- **Regulatory environment** analysis
- **Future growth prospects** evaluation

### 5.2 Starting Point: Company Shortlisting

#### Shortlisting Criteria:
- Top 10 companies by market cap
- Top earning companies
- Top growth companies
- **Industry-specific focus**

#### Filtering Parameters:
- **10-year revenue growth** rate
- **Operating profit margin**
- **Debt ratio** within acceptable range
- Any other prioritized financial ratios

### 5.3 Qualitative Analysis Framework

#### Business Understanding:
- Core business operations
- Different product/service lines
- **Revenue breakdown** by segments
- Business vision and investment horizon
- Geographic presence and stakeholder analysis

#### Research Sources:
> [!resources] Research Sources
> - Google searches and company websites
> - **SEDAR+** website (Canadian companies)
> - **EDGAR** database (US companies)
> - Analyst reports and research
> - **10K filings** (annual audited financial statements)
> - SWOT analysis reports

#### Advanced Research Areas:
- Strategic business priorities
- Company investments and future investment plans
- **Capital expenditures (CAPEX)** for fixed assets
- Financing methods: retained earnings vs. debt
- Management track record and past performance
- **Earnings call transcripts** from investor relations
- Recent news articles and press releases

### 5.4 Quantitative Valuation Techniques

#### 1. Intrinsic Valuation (Absolute Valuation)

##### Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) Model:
> [!important] Most Comprehensive Method
> DCF is considered the most thorough valuation method as it considers all aspects of company value.

- Considers **future cash flows**
- Incorporates **growth rate assumptions**
- Uses **required rate of return** (CAPM)
- Determines if stock is **undervalued or overvalued**

**Components:**
- Free cash flow projections
- Terminal value calculation
- Discount rate determination
- Present value calculation

#### 2. Relative Valuation:
- Compare companies within **same industry**
- **Ratio-based comparisons**
- Industry benchmarking
- Peer group analysis

**Common Ratios:**
- **Price-to-earnings (P/E)**
- **Price-to-book (P/B)**
- **Price-to-sales (P/S)**
- **Enterprise value ratios**

### 5.5 Quantitative Health Check

#### Financial Position Assessment:

##### Liquidity Ratios:
| Ratio | Formula | Purpose |
|-------|---------|---------|
| **Current Ratio** | Current Assets รท Current Liabilities | Short-term obligation coverage |
| **Quick Ratio** | (Current Assets - Inventory) รท Current Liabilities | Immediate liquidity |
| **Cash Ratio** | Cash รท Current Liabilities | Most conservative liquidity |

##### Debt Ratios:
- **Debt-to-equity ratio**
- **Interest coverage ratio**
- **Debt service coverage ratio**
- Financial leverage analysis

##### Growth Metrics:
- **Revenue growth rates**
- **Earnings growth rates**
- Book value growth
- Market share expansion

##### Profitability Ratios:
- **Return on Equity (ROE)**
- **Return on Investment (ROI)**
- **Return on Assets (ROA)**
- **Gross profit margin**
- **Net profit margin**
- **Operating margin**

#### Analysis Methods:
- **Time-series analysis** (historical trends)
- **Industry benchmarking**
- Cross-sectional comparison
- Download Excel format data for analysis
